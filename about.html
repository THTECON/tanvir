Background

I am an aspiring economist and independent researcher based in Dhaka, Bangladesh, with a passion for understanding the forces that shape economies — from monetary policy transmission to the structural realities of labour markets.

I hold a Bachelor of Social Sciences in Economics from East West University. Since January 2026, I have been working as a Research Associate in the Quantitative Section at White Globe Initiative (WGI) — applying econometric and statistical methods to applied research.

My research interests lie at the intersection of macroeconomic analysis, time series econometrics, and experimental research. I am particularly drawn to how monetary policy ripples through developing economies, and how social structures shape labour market outcomes in ways standard models often miss.

I am currently pursuing a Data Analyst Professional Certificate — expanding my toolkit alongside my econometric training.
