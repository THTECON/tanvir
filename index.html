About Me

Who I Am

I am an aspiring economist based in Dhaka, Bangladesh, with a passion for understanding the forces that shape economies — from the transmission of monetary policy to the structural realities of labour markets. I hold a Bachelor of Social Sciences in Economics from East West University, where I built a strong foundation in both economic theory and quantitative methods.

Since January 2026, I have been working as a Research Assistant , at White Globe Inicitave (WGI) — an experience that has sharpened my ability to think rigorously about economic problems and translate them into publishable research.

My research interests lie at the intersection of macroeconomic analysis, time series econometrics, and experimental research methods. I am particularly drawn to questions about how policy decisions — especially monetary policy — ripple through developing economies like Bangladesh, and how individual behaviour and social structures shape labour market outcomes in ways that standard models often miss.

Beyond numbers and regressions, I believe in research that is honest, accessible, and consequential. I am always looking to collaborate, learn, and contribute to conversations that matter.
